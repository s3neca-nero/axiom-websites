Biography

Anthony has spent his career at the intersection of capital markets, technology and Australian industrial policy. Proto Axiom is the application of that experience to biotech — converting world-class Australian science into companies that can stand alongside the best globally.

He co-founded Applecart, the New York-headquartered data-and-technology company, which raised a US$100M investment from Blackstone Growth. He served as a Director of Applecart and was instrumental in scaling the platform across North America before returning to Sydney to launch Proto Axiom.

At Proto Axiom, Anthony has assembled a permanent-capital holding company structure backed by 33 private investors — including TIME Ventures (Marc Benioff), Ray Dalio, Ajay Banga, Catalio Capital/KKR, Reliance Life Sciences and HOF Capital. He sits on the boards of every Proto Axiom portfolio company and chairs the firm's investment committee.

He represents Australia on a number of bilateral and multilateral fora, including as a board member of the Australian American Association and as an advisor to the Australian Government on industrial and innovation policy.

We co-create biotech companies with researchers — from first capital and company formation through preclinical milestones to clinical and commercial reality.

From a 2025 portfolio letter

Anthony graduated from Sydney Grammar School and holds degrees from the Wharton School and Northwestern University. He is a Director of the Liveris Academy at the University of Queensland and a Trustee of Sydney Grammar School.

Biography

Brian is a senior pharmaceutical R&D executive with more than three decades at Merck Research Laboratories, where he led safety assessment and translational sciences across the company's vaccine and gene-therapy portfolios.

He has led preclinical safety programs for more than 40 vaccine candidates, including programs that advanced to licensure across infectious disease and oncology. His group at Merck pioneered approaches to preclinical evaluation of viral-vector and nucleic-acid based therapies, work which has shaped industry-wide regulatory expectations.

Since retiring from Merck, Brian has advised early-stage biotechs and academic spin-outs on translational strategy and regulatory positioning. At Proto Axiom, he chairs the Scientific Advisory Board and leads diligence on incoming research across the Challenger Pitch for Health pipeline.
